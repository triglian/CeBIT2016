Why ASQ?

Why do we need interactive lectures? Although in our journey with ASQ we have found a lot of reasons, the main motivation was due to three reasons: audience engagement, and presenter cognitive load and awareness.

So how to we engage students to educational interactions? We have slides that can have questions for which the users give answers. In more detail, ASQ is a Web platform for interactive HTML slides, that allows you to interleave questions in them and receive answers from your audience in real time. Admittedly, the main job of a presenter is to engage their audience. And we use a lot of ways to do that. Cognitive load

Modern pedagogies like active learning and constructivism dictate interactions with the taught material that go beyond simple multi-choice questions. Educators are faced with higher cognitive load when they need to gather, assess and give feedback for solving equations, programming or describing chemical reactions exercise. And they have to do this while they should still pay attention to the rest of their educational duties like making sure students are engaged, manage classroom time, follow the curriculum and more.
Human performance is produced by controlled and processes Automatic processes happen rapidly occur without intention. They are not subject to conscious monitoring and utilize few, if any, attentional resources. Controlled processes occur in the working memory (a limited resource). They are effortful and slow. These procesess operate independently but intersect at certain points Information processing occurs simultaneously in both pathways. Conflicts are mediated in the working memory which results in a performance drop
